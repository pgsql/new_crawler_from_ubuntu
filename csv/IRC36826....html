Detailed Description
  • Ongoing analysis of Bank overall asset portfolio to assist VP, Treasury in full utilization of Bank assets to secure liquidity and borrowing capacity for the Bank

  • Analysis of specific consumer loan portfolio characteristics to determine ongoing eligibility in support of monthly loan pledging to the Federal Home Loan Bank and the Federal Reserve Bank in order to collateralize credit lines. Will include coordination with both internal parties and external parties who will assist in the identification of eligible loans

  • Analysis and reporting of Bank and corporate available total liquidity, including subsidiaries such as E*TRADE Clearing

  • Analysis of monthly activity affecting the balance sheets of E*TRADE Savings Bank and E*TRADE United Bank in order to assist the VP, Treasury in directing the movement of securities and capital in order to comply with all required liquidity and capital ratios, as well as minimum QTL investment requirements

  • Assist the VP, Treasury in preparing annual budget and monthly forecasts of all funding positions as well as expected cost of funds

  • Run all current liquidity ratio reporting and various liquidity scenario analysis reports

  • Assist VP, Treasury in ongoing analysis of Bank liquidity position against changes which will be required as a result of Basel III or other regulatory change

  • Learn internal funding policies, processes and systems in order to back up the Senior Manager, Treasury in trading and securing borrowings for the Bank. 

  • Counterparty interfacing and management as well as comfort in interacting with multiple internal teams including trading and balance sheet management, risk reporting, credit, audit and accounting
  • Maintaining consistent communication with FP&A and Regulatory Reporting to ensure accuracy of all data around liquidity and funding

  • Ad-hoc analytics and projects as required
Job Requirements
  • Minimum of five years Bank analytics experience, knowledge of  the brokerage and clearing industries is a plus

  • Basic understanding of typical treasury funding vehicles, including repurchase agreements, dollar rolls, brokered certificates of deposit, Fed or Federal Home Loan Bank borrowings

  • Superior Microsoft Excel and PowerPoint knowledge, highly comfortable creating spreadsheets to manage multiple data inputs and create ad-hoc reporting

  • Should be a strong team player with exemplary communication and excellent problem solving skills. 

  • Interested in developing knowledge of current bank regulatory environment and coming regulatory change

  • Independent and self-motivated

  • Must possess strong analytical ability with attention to detail

  • Must have ability to prioritize and multi-task initiatives
